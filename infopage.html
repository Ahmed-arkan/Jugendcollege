Introduction
"1000 chances for 1000 young people" with this goal was launched in the summer of 2016 Start Vienna - the youth college. Educational opportunities for adolescents and young adults between the ages of 15 and 21 are being successfully created at two locations. Vienna, 10.5.2017 More than 1300 young people and young adults between the ages of 15 and 21 have been advised in recent months on their educational background - with the aim of providing participants with secondary education, vocational training or sustainable employment. With some great success: Around 140 graduates have already moved to further education, apprenticeships, studies or employment.
Numbers and Facts
Basically, there are 1002 places available for young adults in the Youth College. 73 percent of them are male, 27 percent are female, and the majority of young people are from Afghanistan (442) and Syria (244). The remaining nationalities are Somalia, Iraq, Iran, Romania, Bulgaria and Serbia. In terms of age, the largest group is the group of 18 year olds, followed by 19 year olds with 22%. Over the past few months, more than 80 young people have successfully been placed and new adolescents have been added to new monthly clearings.
Educational offers for young people at StartWien
The course offer in StartWien - The Youth College comprises the core modules Basic Education (Mathematics, English, Information and Communication Technologies) and German Modules. In addition, there are special modules (eg nature, health and social issues, critical participation, peer interpreting and workshops) that are combined according to level, previous knowledge and educational or vocational educational goals.
Read more Module-related offers, such as socially integrative activities, educational and career counseling and support services complete the offer. The planned average length of stay in the youth college is 9 months. Every 8 weeks, the timetable of the participants changes and thus allows a higher flexibility and individualization of the training.
Educational project funded by the City of Vienna, the AMS and the European Social Fund
Six million euros are spent on this educational project each year, half of which is funded by the European Social Fund (ESF), the other half by the Department for Integration and Diversity MA 17, AMS Vienna and FSW Vienna.
